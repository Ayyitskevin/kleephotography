Booking playbook

Internal reference — the all-in-one booking flow for Kevin Lee Photography, end to end. Not client-facing. Pricing and brand copy in [brackets] are placeholders for Kevin to finalize. Each stage links to the Mise tool that already does the work.

← Studio

This mirrors the strongest HoneyBook all-in-one sample (proposal + invoice + contract + questionnaire in one flow), rebuilt in our own structure. Work top to bottom for a new booking; everything here is assembled from pieces Mise already has, so there is nothing new to wire up — just follow the steps and fill the placeholders.

  1. 1 · Introduction

    "At Kevin Lee Photography, we're not just photographers; we're storytellers." — Our Story voice anchor

    Open with the brand intro: who we are, the F&B / commercial focus, and what it's like to work together. Keep it to headlines and fragments, not essay paragraphs.

    [Paste finalized Our Story intro + About copy here once approved.]

    In Mise: this is the cover note on a Proposal (Studio → open a Project → Proposal). The public services page carries the same voice — templates/site/services.html.

  2. 2 · Proposed package

    Pick the collection that fits the shoot. Existing F&B / commercial / video presets are ready; portrait & brand tiers are scaffolded with placeholder pricing.

    • Photography — Starter / Standard / Premium (live presets)
    • Videography — Starter / Standard / Premium (live presets)
    • Brand Partner (monthly) — Starter / Standard / Premium (live presets)
    • Portrait sessions — [Tier I $___ / Tier II $___ / Tier III $___] placeholder
    • Brand sessions — [Half-day $___ / Full brand package $___] placeholder

    In Mise: Project → Proposal → choose a preset (PRESETS in app/admin/proposals.py). Add the portrait/brand tiers as presets once pricing is set.

  3. 3 · Invoice

    Modeled on the "Simple Invoice" base: itemized services, deposit split, clear schedule.

    Line itemAmount
    [Session / package name]$[___]
    [Add-on — e.g. album, extra setups, social crops]$[___]
    Subtotal$[___]
    Tax (8.75%)$[___]
    Total$[___]

    Payment schedule (suggested): [deposit] due today to hold the date · [balance] due [2 weeks before the shoot] / [on delivery]. Default terms: deposits non-refundable unless stated otherwise.

    In Mise: Project → Invoice — per-project line items (app/admin/invoices.py). Set deposit + due date there.

  4. 4 · Contract

    Attach the agreement that matches the work. All are in the contract library and gated as drafts for attorney review before first use.

    • Photography Services Agreement — standard photo bookings
    • Videography Services Agreement — video deliverables
    • Styled Shoot Photo Release — collaborative / styled shoots with credits
    • Event Contract — on-site events
    • Model Release / Liability Waiver — talent / higher-risk shoots
    • Unilateral NDA — brand/product work under embargo

    In Mise: Project → Contracts → pick a template → review → Send (typed-name e-sign). Library: app/admin/contracts.py.

  5. 5 · Payment

    Client pays the deposit (and later the balance) from the invoice pay page. Status flows back automatically.

    In Mise: Send the invoice → client pays via Stripe Checkout on the public /i/{slug} page → webhook marks it paid → Notion Session props sync (keeps Odysseus balance_chaser / digest accurate).

  6. 6 · Questionnaire

    Send the right intake form so the shoot is dialed in, then a feedback form after delivery.

    • Before the shoot: Client Onboarding · Pre-shoot / Experience questionnaire
    • After delivery: Satisfaction · Videographer Feedback · Request for a Testimonial

    In Mise: Forms — share the public link or use a one-click templated email send.

  7. 7 · Next steps & delivery

    Close the loop: deliver the gallery, capture a testimonial, request feedback.

    In Mise: Galleries for delivery; email templates "Gallery delivery — sharing & credit," "Request for a testimonial," and "Video feedback — your film is ready" for the follow-ups.
